Migration is Spiking Through the Darién Gap

By: Anushka Srivastava

The once impenetrable Darién Gap has in recent years become a junction for migrants seeking a better life up north. The overwhelming majority of them are heading towards the United States.

Only a handful of ecotourists and migrants made their way through the jungle before the 2010s. However, due to violent and economic crises surging around Latin America, and restrictive visa policies, hundreds of thousands of people are risking their lives.
